A note on averages vs. medians in this report: The Jordan Elementary zone includes everything from $400K townhomes to $6M+ Rosebrooke estates. This wide price spread means the average sale price is consistently pulled upward by a small number of ultra-luxury transactions — particularly after 2022 when Rosebrooke began delivering. The median price is the more useful benchmark for understanding what the typical home in this zone is worth. Both are shown throughout so you can see how wide the spread is in any given year.

2022 — Rate Shock Slashed Volume, Not Prices. Transactions fell 46% as mortgage rates spiked from 3% to 7%+, but prices continued climbing — the median hit $1.46M. This is the clearest evidence of how resilient this zone is: even when buyers retreat, the sellers who do transact hold their value. A median DOM of 3 days shows urgency from qualified buyers never really disappeared. Allens Green delivered its first batch of $1.5–2M+ homes this year.

2023 — The Low-Volume Year That Held Its Ground. Only 75 closings — the lowest since before 2019 — as the rate environment suppressed inventory and sidelined move-up buyers. Despite this, median prices held near $1.49M and average DOM stayed reasonable at 15 days median. Rosebrooke's luxury deliveries began skewing average figures upward. The schools, the amenities, and the location meant that motivated sellers in this zone still found motivated buyers.
