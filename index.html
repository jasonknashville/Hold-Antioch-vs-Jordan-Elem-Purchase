How to read this report: This analysis compares two paths for the same ~$900K in equity. Path A keeps the 3 Antioch rentals as-is (or renovated), collecting rent and hoping for appreciation. Path B sells those properties, deploys the equity into a cash purchase in the Jordan Elementary zone (Scales Farmstead or Taramore), and simultaneously generates $3,200/mo from the separate Nolensville rental already owned. All projections use 2% annual appreciation for Antioch and 5% for the Jordan zone — consistent with historical trends in both markets. This is not financial advice; it is a data-informed framework to support your decision.

Why the Jordan Zone Is Different
Market resilience data · 2019–2026 · All neighborhoods zoned for Jordan Elementary
A market that held its value when rate shock hit everywhere else. In 2022, when mortgage rates spiked from 3% to 7%+ and transaction volume across Middle Tennessee fell significantly, the Jordan Elementary zone saw volume drop 46% — but median prices kept climbing from $967K to $1.46M. In 2023, with only 75 closings (the lowest on record), the median still held near $1.49M. This is not typical behavior for a rental market. It is the behavior of a location-driven, school-driven, scarcity-driven market. Antioch does not have this insulation.
📈
Median Price Up 116% Since 2019
Scales Farmstead went from a $600K median to $995K. Taramore went from $852K to $1.72M. Both meaningfully outpaced Davidson County appreciation over the same period.
🛡️
Rate Shock Didn't Break Prices
2022–2023 saw the highest rate environment in 20 years. Volume dropped zone-wide, but prices held. Sellers who waited got their number. That's a quality-of-location story, not a market story.
🏫
School Zone = Demand Floor
Jordan Elementary is one of the most sought-after school assignments in Williamson County. Families relocating to Nashville specifically target this zone — providing a perpetual demand floor that Antioch cannot replicate.
